## AUTHENTICATION NOTES FOR ALL 6 VIDEOS

**Language authenticity**:
- Uses real quotes from Reddit (where applicable)
- Uses real data from peer-reviewed research
- Avoids marketing clichés ("magic," "revolutionary," "game-changer")
- Focuses on emotional truths (frustration, regret, relief, winning)

**Visual authenticity**:
- Real tools shown (Gmail, Slack, Asana, Salesforce, etc.)
- Real scenarios (not stylized/over-produced)
- Real timeline impacts (showing actual day-by-day or week-by-week consequences)
- Real financial impact (showing actual dollar amounts)

**Data sources**:
- Video 1: r/CRM, r/smallbusiness Reddit threads
- Video 2: MIT Lead Response Management study + 9 case studies
- Video 3: Multiple lead scoring ROI studies (138% ROI with lead scoring vs 79% without)
- Video 4: Real campaign performance data showing 48-hour lag impact
- Video 5: Coefficient case study (8 days → 3 days with workflow optimization)
- Video 6: Industry average CRM implementation data (6 weeks, $12K)

**Emotional arc** (across all 6):
1. Problem/Frustration (viewer identifies: "That's me")
2. Cost/Pain (emotional + financial stakes)
3. Competitive threat (fear element)
4. Synthex solution (relief, clarity)
5. Proof (real result)
6. Action (confident CTA)
