# Admin / Brand & Operations Support Role Specification

**Role ID**: admin_brand_ops
**Display Name**: Admin / Brand & Operations Support
**Reports To**: Operations Manager
**Extends**: Base Staff Intelligence Model
**Date**: 2025-11-26

---

## Overview

The Admin / Brand Ops support role is critical for keeping the organization running smoothly. This role manages communication triage, brand asset libraries, scheduling, and administrative workflows. Success here prevents bottlenecks across the entire organization.

---

## Core Duties (From Base Model)

1. Plan daily and weekly work
2. Execute work to standard
3. Provide timely updates
4. Maintain respectful conduct
5. Take ownership & accountability

---

## Admin/Brand-Ops-Specific Duties

### 1. Inbox & Communication Triage

**Frequency**: Daily

**Description**: Monitor shared inboxes, Slack channels, and communication channels. Route messages to the right person, ensure SLAs are met, and escalate urgent issues appropriately.

**Expected Output**: Tagged and triaged messages with clear ownership and due dates

**Tools**: Email, CRM, shared inbox, chat system

**Success Criteria**:
- Low rate of missed or overdue responses (<5% of inbound messages)
- Clear routing with context for the recipient
- SLA achievement (see KPI below)
- No client complaints about delayed response

### 2. Brand Asset Management

**Frequency**: Weekly

**Description**: Maintain up-to-date brand assets, templates, design kits, and content libraries. Ensure staff have easy access to current approved materials.

**Expected Output**: Organized, current brand asset libraries with clear usage guides

**Tools**: Google Drive, Canva, design tools, brand portal

**Success Criteria**:
- Few complaints about outdated brand elements
- Team consistently using approved assets
- Rapid onboarding to brand standards for new staff
- Audit finds 95%+ compliance with brand guidelines

### 3. Calendar & Scheduling Management

**Frequency**: Daily

**Description**: Manage shared calendars, schedule meetings, coordinate team availability, and send calendar invites.

**Expected Output**: Well-organized shared calendars with clear availability and meeting notes

**Tools**: Google Calendar, scheduling tool, meeting notes app

**Success Criteria**:
- Minimal scheduling conflicts
- Meetings have clear agendas and notes
- Staff can easily find free time
- Calendar information is up to date

### 4. Documentation & Knowledge Management

**Frequency**: Ongoing

**Description**: Update and maintain centralized documentation, SOPs, templates, and knowledge base. Ensure staff can find information quickly.

**Expected Output**: Current, searchable knowledge base with clear structure

**Tools**: Documentation platform, wiki, shared drives

**Success Criteria**:
- Staff can find needed info within 5 minutes
- Documentation is up to date with current processes
- New staff can onboard independently using docs
- Low support tickets for "how do I..." questions

## Admin/Brand-Ops-Specific KPIs

### Response SLA Achievement

**Metric Type**: Percentage
**Target**: 95%+
**Warning Threshold**: 85%
**Critical Threshold**: 75%
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.30

**Description**: Percentage of inbound inquiries triaged/acknowledged within agreed response timeframe (typically 4 business hours for routine, 1 hour for urgent).

**Data Source**: Shared inbox metrics, timestamp logs

**Calculation**: (Timely responses) / (Total inbound) × 100

### Brand Asset Accuracy

**Metric Type**: Count
**Target**: 0
**Warning Threshold**: 2
**Critical Threshold**: 5+
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.25

**Description**: Number of internal feedback items about outdated, incorrect, or missing brand assets.

**Data Source**: Feedback forms, spot checks, audit reports

**Calculation**: Count of documented issues per month

### Calendar/Scheduling Efficiency

**Metric Type**: Score (1-10)
**Target**: 8+
**Warning Threshold**: 6.5
**Critical Threshold**: 5
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.20

**Description**: Team feedback on calendar organization, meeting scheduling, and availability visibility.

**Data Source**: Monthly survey, 1-on-1 feedback

**Calculation**: Average response score on scheduling-related questions

### Knowledge Base Usefulness

**Metric Type**: Score (1-10)
**Target**: 7.5+
**Warning Threshold**: 6
**Critical Threshold**: 5
**Period**: Quarterly
**Weight**: 0.15

**Description**: Staff perception of how easily they can find needed information in documentation.

**Data Source**: Survey, support ticket analysis

**Calculation**: Average response to "How easy is it to find what you need in our docs?"

### Documentation Freshness

**Metric Type**: Percentage
**Target**: 95%+
**Warning Threshold**: 85%
**Critical Threshold**: 70%
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.10

**Description**: Percentage of documentation last updated within its required review cycle.

**Data Source**: Documentation system timestamp reviews

**Calculation**: (Current docs) / (Total docs) × 100

## Behavioral Expectations

### Proactive Service Orientation
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Anticipates needs before being asked
  - Solves problems instead of just reporting them
  - Goes extra mile for teammates and clients
  - Maintains positive attitude despite routine work

- **Red Flags**:
  - Waits passively for instructions
  - Reports problems without suggesting solutions
  - Dismissive or impatient with requests
  - Complains about routine aspects of role

### Attention to Detail
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Catches errors before they reach others
  - Maintains organized systems consistently
  - Double-checks important information
  - Follows checklists and standards

- **Red Flags**:
  - Frequent typos, errors, or missed information
  - Disorganized systems causing delays
  - Trusting information without verification
  - Inconsistent application of standards

### Discretion & Confidentiality
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Respects sensitive information
  - Doesn't gossip about clients or staff
  - Handles confidential communications appropriately
  - Follows privacy and data protection protocols

- **Red Flags**:
  - Sharing information outside need-to-know circle
  - Gossip or speculation about clients/staff
  - Careless handling of sensitive documents
  - Privacy breaches or policy violations

## Reporting Structure

**Reports To**: Operations Manager

**Direct Reports**: None (may provide guidance to interns or junior admin)

**Performance Review**: Monthly (brief) + Quarterly (formal)

**Key Relationships**:
- Operations Manager (day-to-day supervisor)
- All team members (service provider)
- Clients (first point of contact for many communications)

---

## Special Considerations

### Impact Despite Non-Decision-Making Role
This role has significant impact despite being a support/admin role. Failures here (missed communications, outdated assets, scheduling conflicts) ripple across the organization.

### High Visibility
This role is often the first contact clients and new staff have with the organization. Brand impression and professionalism are critical.

### Rhythm & Routine
This role thrives on consistency and routine. Success comes from creating and maintaining systems that work reliably.

### Workload Management
Expected workload: 35-40 hours/week

- Inbox triage & routing: 8-10 hours/week
- Brand asset management: 5-7 hours/week
- Calendar & scheduling: 5-7 hours/week
- Documentation: 5-8 hours/week
- Ad-hoc support & escalations: 5-10 hours/week

## Growth Path

This role can develop into:
- Operations Manager (with leadership training)
- Specialist roles (customer success, brand, etc.)
- Project coordination
- Other areas requiring strong organizational/process skills

---

## Success in This Role

The Admin/Brand Ops support person succeeds when:
1. Communication SLAs are consistently met
2. Brand assets are current and easily accessible
3. Team feels supported and not delayed by admin tasks
4. Calendars and scheduling run smoothly
5. New staff can find information and onboard independently
6. Few escalations or complaints about admin support
