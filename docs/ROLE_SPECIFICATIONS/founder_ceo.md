# Founder / CEO Role Specification

**Role ID**: founder_ceo
**Display Name**: Founder / CEO
**Reports To**: Board or trusted external advisor
**Extends**: Base Staff Intelligence Model
**Date**: 2025-11-26

---

## Overview

The Founder / CEO role is unique. Rather than executing tasks delegated by others, the Founder sets direction, approves major decisions, and provides overall oversight.

The Founder is the **single point of truth** for:
- Strategic direction and priorities
- Approval of public-facing claims (truth layer enforcement)
- AI and automation scope and boundaries
- Major business and personnel decisions

---

## Core Duties (From Base Model)

From the Base Staff Intelligence Model, the Founder is expected to:

1. **Plan daily and weekly work** - Set personal priorities and strategic focus
2. **Provide timely updates** - Keep board/advisors informed
3. **Maintain respectful conduct** - Model behavior for the organization
4. **Take ownership** - For outcomes, not just actions

---

## Founder-Specific Duties

### 1. Set Vision & Strategic Direction

**Frequency**: Quarterly (with ongoing refinement)

**Description**: Define and refine the long-term direction of Unite-Hub Nexus and all related brands. This includes market positioning, product roadmap, growth targets, and strategic priorities.

**Expected Output**: Updated strategic roadmap with clear priorities, trade-offs, and resource allocation.

**Tools**: Strategic documents, CRM dashboards, financial reports, team input

**Success Criteria**:
- Roadmap aligns with team capacity and available resources
- Strategic priorities are reflected in project pipeline and sprint planning
- Team members can repeat the key direction in simple terms
- Board/advisors agree the direction is sound and achievable

### 2. AI Orchestration & Oversight

**Frequency**: Weekly review + ad-hoc for major changes

**Description**: Oversee all autonomous systems (Phase 4 Execution Engine, Phase 5 Agents), ensure safety constraints are maintained, and approve any changes to AI policies, automation scopes, or agent boundaries.

**Expected Output**: Approved/rejected changes to AI policies, documented safety reviews, clear reasoning for major decisions.

**Tools**: AI Orchestration Dashboard, audit logs, agent status pages

**Success Criteria**:
- No major incidents caused by unsupervised AI actions
- Documented reviews of all significant automation changes
- Clear risk/benefit reasoning for major AI decisions
- Safety constraints are maintained and auditable

### 3. Truth Layer Enforcement

**Frequency**: Ongoing (especially for public-facing content)

**Description**: Ensure all public claims, testimonials, results, and promises are truthful, backed by data, and not misleading. This is a non-delegable responsibility.

**Expected Output**: Approved content, identified inaccuracies, guidance on claim substantiation

**Tools**: Content review workflows, data dashboards, marketing materials

**Success Criteria**:
- Zero fake testimonials in public output
- Zero unsubstantiated results claims
- All scarcity claims backed by fact (not artificial urgency)
- AI usage disclosed where appropriate
- Regular audits of public claims pass internal review

### 4. Personnel & Performance Decisions

**Frequency**: Ongoing (quarterly reviews, ad-hoc issues)

**Description**: Make final decisions on hiring, performance issues, discipline, and personnel changes. Work with Operations Manager on preparation, but Founder has final say.

**Expected Output**: Documented decisions with clear reasoning, aligned with policies

**Tools**: HR files, performance reviews, interview notes, CRM

**Success Criteria**:
- Fair, consistent decisions across team
- Clear communication of reasoning to affected staff
- Low turnover of high performers
- Compliance with employment law

## Founder-Specific KPIs

### System Health & Stability Score

**Metric Type**: Score (0-100)
**Target**: 90+
**Warning Threshold**: 80
**Critical Threshold**: 70
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.30

**Description**: Composite score covering uptime, incident rate, client-impacting issues, and system reliability.

**Data Source**: Monitoring dashboards, incident logs, customer feedback

**Calculation**: Weighted average of:
- Uptime (target: 99.9%)
- Mean time to resolution for incidents
- Client-reported issues (target: 0 critical, <5 non-critical per month)
- Security incidents (target: 0)

### Strategic Execution Rate

**Metric Type**: Percentage
**Target**: 80%
**Warning Threshold**: 65%
**Critical Threshold**: 50%
**Period**: Quarterly
**Weight**: 0.30

**Description**: Percentage of strategic initiatives planned in roadmap that are delivered on time and to scope.

**Data Source**: Project portfolio, sprint completion, roadmap tracking

**Calculation**: (Completed on-time, on-scope initiatives) / (Total planned initiatives) Ã— 100

### AI Safety Incident Rate

**Metric Type**: Count
**Target**: 0
**Warning Threshold**: 1
**Critical Threshold**: 2+
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.20

**Description**: Number of significant incidents caused by AI systems operating outside founder-approved scope or breaking safety constraints.

**Data Source**: Incident logs, audit trails

**Calculation**: Count of incidents where root cause is autonomous system behavior outside policy

### Brand Health & Coherence

**Metric Type**: Score (0-100)
**Target**: 85+

**Period**: Quarterly
**Weight**: 0.10

**Description**: Composite measure of brand alignment across all properties, including positioning consistency, messaging accuracy, and cross-brand confusion incidents.

**Data Source**: Brand audits, customer feedback, content reviews

**Calculation**: Blend of positioning consistency score, content accuracy audit, and incident tracking

### Team Alignment & Engagement

**Metric Type**: Score (0-100)
**Target**: 80+

**Critical Threshold**: 60
**Period**: Quarterly
**Weight**: 0.10

**Description**: Measure of team understanding of direction, engagement with strategy, and psychological safety.

**Data Source**: Team surveys, 1-on-1 feedback, exit interviews

**Calculation**: Average of alignment, engagement, and safety scores from quarterly survey

## Behavioral Expectations

Beyond base model standards, Founder is expected to:

### Transparent Decision-Making
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Explains reasoning behind decisions
  - Listens to team input before deciding
  - Adjusts course based on new evidence
  - Admits when decisions didn't work out

- **Red Flags**:
  - Unexplained reversals of direction
  - Dismissing team input without consideration
  - Doubling down on failed strategies
  - Blame-shifting when things don't work

### Modeling of Values
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Demonstrates respectful conduct toward all
  - Admits mistakes and learns from them
  - Shows genuine interest in team development
  - Protects confidentiality and privacy

- **Red Flags**:
  - Treating certain staff as less valuable
  - Using confidential information inappropriately
  - Undermining managers in front of team
  - Different behavior in public vs. private

### Decisive Leadership
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Makes timely decisions even with imperfect information
  - Provides clear direction to teams
  - Takes responsibility for outcomes
  - Supports decisions once made

- **Red Flags**:
  - Analysis paralysis leading to missed opportunities
  - Frequent reversals causing whiplash
  - Blaming others for failures
  - Constantly second-guessing team decisions

## Reporting Structure (Unique)

The Founder / CEO role has a different reporting structure:

**Founder Accountability**:
- To: Board of Directors or trusted external advisor
- Performance Review: Annual (conducted by external party or board)
- Key Relationships: Co-founder, board members, key advisors

**Note**: The Founder is not disciplined through the standard 5-level process. If serious governance concerns arise, they would be addressed by the board.

---

## Special Considerations

### Decision Authority
The Founder has final decision authority on:
- Strategic direction and roadmap
- AI policy and automation scope
- Personnel decisions (hiring, firing, discipline)
- Public-facing claims and truth layer compliance
- Major financial and legal commitments

### Delegation vs. Authority
The Founder delegates execution to the Operations Manager, department heads, and teams. But:
- Founder retains oversight responsibility
- Founder must approve policy changes
- Founder cannot abdicate truth layer enforcement
- Founder bears ultimate accountability

### Workload Management
The Founder's duties are high-level but significant. Workload management includes:
- Quarterly strategic planning (15-20 hours)
- Weekly oversight and reviews (3-5 hours)
- Monthly board/advisor sync (2-3 hours)
- Ad-hoc decision-making and approvals (5-10 hours/week)

**Total expected**: 30-40 hours/week of founder-specific duties (leaving room for delegation, learning, and personal development)

---

## Success in This Role

The Founder succeeds when:
1. Strategic initiatives are delivered on time and within scope
2. Systems are stable and reliable (99.9% uptime)
3. Team is engaged, aligned, and growing
4. Brand and public reputation remain strong
5. All public claims are truthful and substantiated
6. AI systems operate safely within approved scopes
7. Decisions are made with clear reasoning and founder accountability

---

## Support & Resources

The Founder should have access to:
- Board or advisory board for strategic counsel
- Operations Manager for day-to-day execution
- HR/Legal advisor for personnel and compliance matters
- Financial advisor or CFO for strategic financial planning
- Tech lead for infrastructure and AI oversight
