# Field Technician Role Specification

**Role ID**: technician_field
**Display Name**: Field Technician
**Reports To**: Operations Manager
**Extends**: Base Staff Intelligence Model
**Date**: 2025-11-26

---

## Overview

The Field Technician performs hands-on work at client sites (inspections, assessments, installations, repairs). Success depends on technical skill, safety compliance, quality of work, and client relationships.

---

## Core Duties (From Base Model)

1. Plan daily and weekly work
2. Execute work to standard
3. Provide timely updates
4. Maintain respectful conduct
5. Take ownership & accountability

---

## Field-Technician-Specific Duties

### 1. Onsite Safety Checks

**Frequency**: Per job

**Description**: Perform safety checks on arrival and before starting work, following SWMS (Safe Work Method Statements) and WHS requirements. Document all hazards and controls.

**Expected Output**: Completed safety checklist, hazard log, documented controls

**Tools**: FieldApp, SWMS, safety forms, hazard logs

**Success Criteria**:
- No avoidable safety incidents
- All jobs have completed safety checklist before starting
- Hazards are identified and controlled
- No WHS violations or compliance issues

### 2. Job Documentation & Photos

**Frequency**: Per job

**Description**: Capture clear photos (before/during/after), take readings (moisture, dimensions, etc.), and write detailed notes to support reports, quotes, and compliance.

**Expected Output**: Complete job files with photos, readings, notes, signatures

**Tools**: FieldApp with camera, moisture meters, measuring tools, note templates

**Success Criteria**:
- Minimal re-attendance due to missing or unclear data
- Photos are clear and show relevant details
- Readings are accurate and properly recorded
- Office/Admin can complete reports without chasing technician for info

### 3. Quality Workmanship

**Frequency**: Per job

**Description**: Execute work to contracted specification, following industry standards and company quality guidelines.

**Expected Output**: Completed work that passes inspection and satisfies client

**Tools**: Tools of trade, instruction guides, quality checklists

**Success Criteria**:
- Few client complaints about quality
- Minimal rework required
- Work passes inspections
- Client satisfaction scores are high

### 4. Client Communication

**Frequency**: Per job

**Description**: Communicate professionally with clients throughout job, explain work clearly, answer questions, and handle concerns.

**Expected Output**: Professional interactions, client understanding, documented feedback

**Tools**: Phone, email, job notes in CRM

**Success Criteria**:
- Clients feel respected and informed
- Misunderstandings are rare
- Positive feedback in post-job surveys
- Few complaints about communication

## Field-Technician-Specific KPIs

### Job Completion Quality

**Metric Type**: Score (0-10)
**Target**: 8+
**Warning Threshold**: 6.5
**Critical Threshold**: 5
**Period**: Per-job aggregated monthly
**Weight**: 0.40

**Description**: Internal rating of job quality and completeness based on inspection, client feedback, and rework requirements.

**Data Source**: Post-job audits, client surveys, rework logs

**Calculation**: Average quality score across all jobs completed in month

### Safety Compliance Rate

**Metric Type**: Percentage
**Target**: 100%
**Warning Threshold**: 95%
**Critical Threshold**: 90%
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.30

**Description**: Percentage of jobs with full safety documentation completed (checklist, hazard log, controls documented).

**Data Source**: FieldApp_Safety_Forms

**Calculation**: (Jobs with complete safety docs) / (Total jobs) × 100

### Rework Rate

**Metric Type**: Percentage
**Target**: <5%
**Warning Threshold**: 5-10%
**Critical Threshold**: 10%+
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.15

**Description**: Percentage of jobs requiring re-attendance due to incomplete or incorrect work.

**Data Source**: Rework logs, client complaints

**Calculation**: (Jobs requiring rework) / (Total jobs) × 100

### Client Satisfaction Score

**Metric Type**: Score (1-10)
**Target**: 8+
**Warning Threshold**: 7
**Critical Threshold**: 6
**Period**: Monthly (aggregated from post-job surveys)
**Weight**: 0.10

**Description**: Average client satisfaction based on post-job surveys covering professionalism, quality, communication.

**Data Source**: Client surveys

**Calculation**: Average response to "How satisfied were you with this technician?"

### Time Estimates Accuracy

**Metric Type**: Percentage
**Target**: 90%+ within 15 minutes
**Warning Threshold**: 80%
**Critical Threshold**: 70%
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.05

**Description**: Percentage of jobs completed within estimated timeframe (±15 minutes).

**Data Source**: Time tracking, job estimates

**Calculation**: (Jobs completed within ±15 min of estimate) / (Total jobs) × 100

## Behavioral Expectations

### Safety-First Mindset
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Never skips safety steps even when rushing
  - Reports hazards proactively
  - Wears PPE consistently
  - Follows SWMS every time

- **Red Flags**:
  - Cutting corners on safety
  - Ignoring hazards to save time
  - PPE not used or used incorrectly
  - Dismissive attitude toward safety

### Professionalism & Client Respect
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Communicates clearly with clients
  - Respects client property and privacy
  - Professional appearance and conduct
  - Handles difficult clients gracefully

- **Red Flags**:
  - Dismissive or rude to clients
  - Damage to client property
  - Unprofessional appearance
  - Defensiveness when issues arise

### Quality Orientation
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Takes pride in work quality
  - Double-checks own work
  - Meets or exceeds specifications
  - Learns from feedback

- **Red Flags**:
  - "Good enough" attitude
  - Rushing through jobs
  - Frequent mistakes
  - Defensive about quality feedback

### Attention to Detail
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Complete documentation
  - Accurate measurements and notes
  - Clear, usable photos
  - Nothing left out of files

- **Red Flags**:
  - Incomplete or missing documentation
  - Inaccurate data
  - Photos that don't show the work
  - Gaps requiring follow-up from office

## Reporting Structure

**Reports To**: Operations Manager

**Direct Reports**: None (may supervise apprentices or junior technicians)

**Performance Review**: Monthly (brief) + Quarterly (formal)

**Key Relationships**:
- Operations Manager (scheduling, support)
- Office/Admin (documentation, reports)
- Clients (direct service relationship)
- Safety Officer or WHS delegate (safety compliance)

---

## Special Considerations

### Safety is Non-Negotiable
This role works in potentially hazardous environments. Safety compliance is not a negotiable KPI—it's a requirement for employment. Any serious safety violation triggers immediate review.

### Physical Demands
This role requires:
- Physical stamina (standing, lifting, climbing)
- Ability to work in various weather conditions
- Manual dexterity and attention to detail
- Willingness to work at heights or in confined spaces (with training)

### Tools & Equipment
The technician is responsible for:
- Maintaining personal tools in good condition
- Using company equipment correctly
- Reporting equipment issues immediately
- Protecting tools from damage or theft

### Travel & Scheduling
- Expect variable schedule based on client appointments
- Travel between job sites
- Possible early mornings or evening appointments
- Weather can affect scheduling

### Continuous Learning
- Keep up with industry standards and changes
- Participate in safety training and certifications
- Learn new techniques and procedures
- Share knowledge with apprentices/junior staff

### Workload Management
Expected workload: 40-45 hours/week

- On-site work: 30-35 hours/week
- Travel between jobs: 3-5 hours/week
- Documentation & reporting: 2-3 hours/week
- Training & safety meetings: 2-3 hours/week

---

## Growth Path

This role can develop into:
- Lead Technician or Team Lead (supervising other technicians)
- Specialist Technician (deep expertise in specific areas)
- Trainer (teaching apprentices and junior staff)
- Operations/Scheduling (moving to coordination role)
- Management track (if interested in leadership)

---

## Safety Certification Requirements

The technician may need certifications depending on work type:
- First Aid & CPR
- Height work certification (if working at height)
- Confined space entry (if applicable)
- Equipment operation certifications
- WHS induction and site-specific training

The company supports maintaining current certifications.

---

## Success in This Role

The Field Technician succeeds when:
1. All jobs are completed safely with zero incidents
2. Quality standards are consistently met
3. Clients are satisfied and provide positive feedback
4. Documentation is complete and accurate
5. Rework is rare (and the technician learns from any issues)
6. Team feels supported by their professionalism
7. Time estimates are consistently accurate
