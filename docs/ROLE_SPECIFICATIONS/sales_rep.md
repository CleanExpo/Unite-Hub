# Sales Representative Role Specification

**Role ID**: sales_rep
**Display Name**: Sales Representative
**Reports To**: Operations Manager (Sales Lead)
**Extends**: Base Staff Intelligence Model
**Date**: 2025-11-26

---

## Overview

The Sales Representative drives revenue by identifying, qualifying, and closing opportunities. Success is measured by new revenue, pipeline quality, and customer acquisition.

---

## Core Duties (From Base Model)

1. Plan daily and weekly work
2. Execute work to standard
3. Provide timely updates
4. Maintain respectful conduct
5. Take ownership & accountability

---

## Sales-Specific Duties

### 1. Pipeline Management

**Frequency**: Daily

**Description**: Maintain accurate lead, opportunity, and deal stages in the CRM. Update forecast, next steps, and decision criteria.

**Expected Output**: Updated pipeline, correct stages, clear next steps on each opportunity, accurate forecast

**Tools**: CRM_Sales, email, phone

**Success Criteria**:
- Minimal "stale" deals (deals not updated in 2+ weeks)
- Accurate forecast vs actuals (within 10%)
- Clear, documented next steps on every active deal
- Pipeline populated 3+ months out

### 2. Discovery & Qualification Calls

**Frequency**: Ongoing (target: 5-10 per week)

**Description**: Run structured discovery calls using agreed scripts and qualification frameworks. Identify fit, budget, authority, need, timeline (BANT).

**Expected Output**: Discovery notes, clear qualification result, next-step plan documented in CRM

**Tools**: Phone, Zoom, CRM_Notes, call scripts

**Success Criteria**:
- High conversion from discovery to qualified opportunity (50%+)
- Low rate of poorly qualified deals entering pipeline (<10%)
- Complete discovery notes for every call
- Time tracking for activity metrics

### 3. Proposal Development & Negotiation

**Frequency**: As deals progress

**Description**: Work with Operations/Finance to develop proposals, pricing, and terms. Negotiate to close deals while protecting company margins.

**Expected Output**: Proposals sent, terms documented, deals closed or moved forward

**Tools**: Proposal templates, pricing tool, CRM

**Success Criteria**:
- Proposals sent within 2 business days of request
- Negotiation stays within approved parameters
- Contracts signed and executed
- Post-sale handoff to support/delivery

## Sales-Specific KPIs

### Monthly New Revenue

**Metric Type**: Currency (AUD)
**Target**: $10,000
**Warning Threshold**: $7,000
**Critical Threshold**: $5,000
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.40

**Description**: Total new recurring or project revenue closed in the month. This is the primary sales metric.

**Data Source**: CRM_Deals, signed contracts

**Calculation**: Sum of deal values where contract signed in month

### Pipeline Hygiene Score

**Metric Type**: Score (0-100)
**Target**: 85+
**Warning Threshold**: 70
**Critical Threshold**: 60
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.25

**Description**: Composite score reflecting accuracy, freshness, and completeness of pipeline data.

**Data Source**: CRM_Audit

**Calculation**: Blend of:
- Data freshness (deals updated in past 2 weeks): 50%
- Completeness (all required fields filled): 30%
- Accuracy (forecast vs actuals): 20%

### Opportunity Qualification Rate

**Metric Type**: Percentage
**Target**: 50%+
**Warning Threshold**: 40%
**Critical Threshold**: 25%
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.15

**Description**: Percentage of discovered leads that become qualified opportunities (move past discovery to active pursuit).

**Data Source**: CRM activity logs

**Calculation**: (Qualified opportunities created) / (Discovery calls completed) Ã— 100

### Sales Cycle Efficiency

**Metric Type**: Days
**Target**: 30-45 days
**Warning Threshold**: 45-60 days
**Critical Threshold**: 60+ days
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.10

**Description**: Average time from first contact to closed deal.

**Data Source**: CRM timestamps

**Calculation**: Average (close date - first contact date) for all closed deals

### Customer Acquisition Cost Ratio

**Metric Type**: Ratio
**Target**: 3:1 (sales cost : customer lifetime value)
**Warning Threshold**: 2:1
**Critical Threshold**: 1:1
**Period**: Quarterly
**Weight**: 0.10

**Description**: Ratio of sales costs to expected lifetime value of acquired customer.

**Data Source**: Finance, sales tracking

**Calculation**: (Sales cost for customer) / (Expected LTV) ratio

## Behavioral Expectations

### Integrity & Honesty
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Sets realistic expectations with clients
  - Doesn't exaggerate or misrepresent capabilities
  - Keeps promises made to clients
  - Admits mistakes and fixes them

- **Red Flags**:
  - Over-promising features or timelines
  - Misleading claims about capabilities
  - Breaking commitments to clients
  - Shifting blame to other teams

### Resilience & Positivity
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Handles rejection professionally
  - Bounces back from lost deals quickly
  - Maintains enthusiasm despite setbacks
  - Motivates team members

- **Red Flags**:
  - Dwelling on lost deals
  - Blaming clients for "wrong" decisions
  - Negative attitude toward prospects
  - Discouraging tone with colleagues

### Customer Focus
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Listens carefully to client needs
  - Proposes solutions that fit the client (not biggest deal)
  - Follows up reliably
  - Builds long-term relationships

- **Red Flags**:
  - Pushing products clients don't need
  - One-time transactional approach
  - Ghosting after sale
  - Ignoring client concerns

## Reporting Structure

**Reports To**: Operations Manager (Sales Lead if available)

**Direct Reports**: None

**Performance Review**: Monthly (brief check-in) + Quarterly (formal review)

**Key Relationships**:
- Operations Manager (supervision, coaching)
- Finance/Ops (deal structure support)
- Delivery/Support teams (post-sale handoff)
- Clients (direct relationship building)

## Special Considerations

### Pipeline Visibility
Pipeline is public (visible to Operations Manager and leadership). This creates transparency and accountability.

### Forecast Accuracy
Forecast accuracy is important for company planning. Sales reps are expected to be realistic about pipeline and close probabilities.

### Deal Protection
Large deals (>$20k) require review and approval before committing to terms.

### Customer Success Handoff
Sale isn't complete until customer is successfully onboarded to delivery/support team.

### Workload Management
Expected workload: 40-45 hours/week

- Discovery calls & prospecting: 15-20 hours/week
- Pipeline management & admin: 5-8 hours/week
- Proposal development: 5-8 hours/week
- Training & development: 2-3 hours/week
- Team/management meetings: 3-5 hours/week

---

## Growth Path

This role can develop into:
- Sales Lead / Sales Manager (with team leadership training)
- Account Executive (managing larger, more complex deals)
- Sales Operations (moving to process/systems role)
- Business Development (strategic partnership focus)

---

## Success in This Role

The Sales Representative succeeds when:
1. Monthly revenue targets are consistently met
2. Pipeline quality is high (good conversion rates)
3. Customers are happy and renew (low churn from sales mistakes)
4. Pipeline is clean and forecast is accurate
5. Discovery process is followed consistently
6. Team members respect their professionalism and integrity
