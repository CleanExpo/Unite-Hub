# Operations Manager Role Specification

**Role ID**: operations_manager
**Display Name**: Operations Manager
**Reports To**: Founder / CEO
**Extends**: Base Staff Intelligence Model
**Date**: 2025-11-26

---

## Overview

The Operations Manager ensures the business runs smoothly day-to-day, teams are resourced appropriately, processes are followed, and KPIs are met. They are the primary point of contact for implementing founder direction.

---

## Core Duties (From Base Model)

1. Plan daily and weekly work
2. Execute work to standard
3. Provide timely updates
4. Maintain respectful conduct
5. Take ownership & accountability

---

## Operations-Manager-Specific Duties

### 1. Resource & Capacity Planning

**Frequency**: Weekly

**Description**: Ensure the right people and tools are available to deliver current and upcoming work, with appropriate buffer for unexpected issues.

**Expected Output**: Capacity plan vs workload, identified gaps, and action plans

**Tools**: CRM, scheduling, FinanceView, project portfolio

**Success Criteria**:
- Minimal overbooking (no more than 10% of weeks)
- Minimal underutilization (resource utilization 70-85%)
- Few last-minute crises due to planning failures
- Team feels supported and resourced

### 2. SOP & Process Enforcement

**Frequency**: Ongoing

**Description**: Ensure staff are using current, approved SOPs and templates in daily work. This includes training, monitoring, and coaching toward compliance.

**Expected Output**: SOP compliance tracking, audit reports, corrective actions

**Tools**: SOPLibrary, CRM audits, process metrics

**Success Criteria**:
- High SOP compliance rate (target: 90%+)
- Low variance in how recurring work is done
- New staff onboarded to current SOPs quickly
- Documented updates when processes change

### 3. Team Performance Management

**Frequency**: Ongoing (monthly summaries, quarterly formal reviews)

**Description**: Monitor team KPIs, identify performance gaps early, coach for improvement, and escalate serious issues to Founder.

**Expected Output**: Monthly performance summaries, 1-on-1 coaching notes, formal review documents

**Tools**: CRM dashboards, KPI trackers, review templates

**Success Criteria**:
- Team KPIs trending toward targets
- Performance issues identified early
- Clear development paths for each team member
- Low surprise failures at formal review time

### 4. Tools & Systems Management

**Frequency**: Ongoing

**Description**: Oversee the CRM, project management tools, documentation systems, and other operational infrastructure. Ensure they're working and staff know how to use them.

**Expected Output**: System uptime, training documentation, problem resolution

**Tools**: CRM admin, system monitoring, knowledge base

**Success Criteria**:
- System uptime 99%+
- Minimal support tickets for tool usage issues
- Rapid issue resolution when problems occur

## Operations-Specific KPIs

### Team KPI Adherence

**Metric Type**: Percentage
**Target**: 85%
**Warning Threshold**: 75%
**Critical Threshold**: 65%
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.30

**Description**: Average KPI performance across all direct reports (weighted average of all KPIs for all team members).

**Data Source**: Aggregated individual KPIs

**Calculation**: Average of all team member KPI achievement scores

### Operational Incident Rate

**Metric Type**: Count
**Target**: 0-1 per month
**Warning Threshold**: 3
**Critical Threshold**: 5+
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.25

**Description**: Number of preventable operational incidents (system failures, process breakdowns, missed deadlines, quality failures).

**Data Source**: Incident logs, post-mortems

**Calculation**: Count of documented incidents with ops root cause

### SOP Compliance Rate

**Metric Type**: Percentage
**Target**: 90%+
**Warning Threshold**: 80%
**Critical Threshold**: 70%
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.20

**Description**: Percentage of work items completed using current approved SOPs and templates.

**Data Source**: CRM audits, spot checks

**Calculation**: (Work items completed per SOP) / (Total work items) × 100

### Team Satisfaction Score

**Metric Type**: Score (1-10)
**Target**: 7.5+
**Warning Threshold**: 6.5
**Critical Threshold**: 5.5
**Period**: Quarterly
**Weight**: 0.15

**Description**: Team survey measuring satisfaction with management, tools, communication, and growth support.

**Data Source**: Anonymous quarterly survey

**Calculation**: Average response to key questions about operations management

### Resource Utilization Rate

**Metric Type**: Percentage
**Target**: 75-85%
**Warning Threshold**: 60% (underutilization) or 90%+ (overutilization)
**Critical Threshold**: <50% or 95%+
**Period**: Monthly
**Weight**: 0.10

**Description**: Percentage of available staff hours actually used on billable/value-adding work.

**Data Source**: Time tracking, project allocation

**Calculation**: (Billable hours) / (Available hours) × 100

## Behavioral Expectations

### Fair & Consistent Leadership
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Treats all team members with equal respect
  - Applies policies consistently
  - Listens to team input on process changes
  - Admits when decisions need to change

- **Red Flags**:
  - Favoritism toward certain team members
  - Inconsistent enforcement of policies
  - Dismissing team feedback
  - Blaming team for issues caused by poor planning

### Coaching Mindset
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Provides constructive feedback quickly
  - Supports team members in developing skills
  - Celebrates wins and learns from failures
  - Invests time in team development

- **Red Flags**:
  - Vague criticism without specifics
  - Punitive approach instead of coaching
  - Ignoring development opportunities
  - Publicly criticizing team members

### Problem-Solving Orientation
- **Positive Indicators**:
  - Identifies systemic issues causing incidents
  - Proposes process improvements
  - Removes obstacles for teams
  - Takes action to prevent recurrence

- **Red Flags**:
  - Focusing on blame instead of solutions
  - Ignoring systemic problems
  - Creating new obstacles through poor planning
  - Repeating same mistakes

## Reporting Structure

**Reports To**: Founder / CEO

**Direct Reports**: All operational staff (sales, admin, technicians, etc.)

**Performance Review**: Quarterly (by Founder/CEO)

**Key Relationships**:
- Founder/CEO (strategy alignment)
- Department leads/specialists (coaching and support)
- All team members (daily management)

---

## Special Considerations

### Decision Authority
The Operations Manager has authority to:
- Assign tasks and manage daily workflow
- Implement approved processes and SOPs
- Make hiring recommendations (with founder approval)
- Allocate resources within approved budget
- Escalate issues to Founder

The Operations Manager does NOT have authority to:
- Change strategic direction
- Approve AI policy changes
- Make major personnel decisions (hiring, firing, discipline level 3+)
- Modify core processes without founder approval

### Founder Support
The Operations Manager is the Founder's primary lever for day-to-day execution. Success for the Operations Manager means:
- Founder can focus on strategy and oversight
- Teams are resourced and supported
- KPIs are tracked and communicated
- Issues are escalated appropriately

### Workload Management
Expected workload: 40-45 hours/week

- Team management & coaching: 15-20 hours/week
- KPI tracking and reporting: 5-7 hours/week
- Process improvement & auditing: 5-8 hours/week
- Escalations & problem-solving: 5-10 hours/week

---

## Success in This Role

The Operations Manager succeeds when:
1. Team KPIs are trending toward targets
2. Operational incidents are rare and handled well
3. Processes are followed consistently
4. Staff feel supported and coached
5. Resources are used efficiently
6. Issues are escalated appropriately to Founder
7. Day-to-day operations run smoothly without founder intervention
