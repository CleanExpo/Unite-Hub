## Recommended first category

### 1. Carpet cleaning machines and extractors

Why this should go first:

- Strongest alignment with the EOFY campaign: machines are high-consideration purchases where timing, stock availability, suitability, and accountant-safe planning language matter.
- Supports Toby conversations without promising tax outcomes: customers can be invited to discuss job fit, stock, servicing, and accountant timing before 30 June.
- Creates a category template that can later be reused for accessories, consumables, chemicals, and servicing pages/posts.
- Likely highest commercial leverage compared with lower-consideration consumables, while still requiring Toby/stock verification before publication.

Draft positioning:

> Practical carpet cleaning machines and extractors for working operators who need reliable gear, fit-for-purpose advice, and local support before and after purchase.

Safe CTAs:

- Talk to CCW about what machine suits your work.
- Ask what is available before EOFY.
- Speak with your accountant about purchase timing before 30 June.
- Book a conversation before buying the wrong machine.

Required Toby inputs before final copy:

- Approved machine/product names.
- Current stock status and lead times.
- Any machines to avoid promoting.
- Warranty/service support wording.
- Preferred CTA path: phone, form, email, Facebook message, or store visit.
- Whether finance/payment options may be mentioned.

## Backup category options

### 2. Chemicals and consumables

Use when Toby wants lower-friction repeat-purchase copy first.

Strengths:

- Easy to connect to EOFY stock-up messaging.
- Lower claims risk if copy stays generic and avoids chemical performance promises.
- Useful for Facebook and email replenishment prompts.

Risks / missing inputs:

- Requires exact product families, safety constraints, dilution/use guidance, and stock list.
- Avoid promising stain/remediation outcomes without product-specific approved wording.

### 3. Accessories, hoses, tools, and spare parts

Use when Toby wants practical replacement/backup gear copy first.

Strengths:

- Connects well to downtime prevention and "replace worn gear" messaging.
- Good bridge between product category copy and service-booking prompts.

Risks / missing inputs:

- Needs approved category names and product examples.
- Avoid implying all accessories fit all machines.

### 4. Workshop servicing and repairs

Use when the priority is service bookings rather than ecommerce/category browsing.

Strengths:

- Strong alignment with mechanics/workshop demand from UNI-2055.
- Easy to position around reliability, maintenance, and booking early before busy periods.

Risks / missing inputs:

- Needs workshop capacity, booking windows, service inclusions/exclusions, and warranty boundaries.
- May be better as a service landing page than a product category.

## Reusable category copy structure

Use this structure once Toby selects the first category:

1. Category headline
2. One-sentence operator outcome
3. Fit-for-purpose buying guidance
4. What CCW can help with
5. EOFY/accountant-safe note where relevant
6. Product/category examples only after Toby confirms real stock
7. Support/service note
8. CTA
9. Compliance/approval checklist

## Draft copy skeleton for recommended first category

Working title:

> Carpet Cleaning Machines and Extractors

Draft intro:

> Choosing a carpet cleaning machine is not just about buying the biggest unit on the shelf. The right machine depends on the type of work you do, how often you use it, the team operating it, van space, maintenance expectations, and what support you need after purchase.

Draft body:

> CCW can help working cleaners compare practical machine options, check current availability, and talk through what suits the jobs you are actually doing. If you are planning an equipment purchase before EOFY, start the conversation early so there is time to review stock, ask questions, and speak with your accountant about timing before 30 June.

Draft support note:

> The CCW team can also help you think through servicing, accessories, backup gear, and setup requirements before you commit to a machine.

Draft CTA:

> Talk to Toby and the CCW team about carpet cleaning machines and extractors that suit your work.

Image/video brief:

- Toby or CCW team member beside machine stock.
- Short walkthrough comparing two machine types without making unsupported performance claims.
- Close-ups of practical details: hoses, controls, size, van fit, service access.

Compliance notes:

- Do not claim guaranteed tax deductions, credits, write-offs, ROI, or business savings.
- Do not claim a machine is best-in-class unless Toby supplies approved evidence.
- Do not mention exact price, availability, finance, warranty, or delivery timing until Toby confirms.
- Use "speak with your accountant" and "before EOFY" only as planning language.
