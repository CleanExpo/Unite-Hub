## H2: What Is Division 7A?

### H3: The Legislative Background (200 words)
- Introduced 1997 as anti-avoidance measure
- Part of ITAA 1936, Sections 109B–109ZB
- Purpose: prevent shareholders/associates extracting company profits tax-free disguised as "loans" or "payments"
- Key point: ignorance is not a defence — deemed dividends are automatic

### H3: What Triggers a Deemed Dividend (250 words)
- Loans from private company to shareholder or associate
- Payments made on behalf of a shareholder
- Debt forgiveness
- Use of company assets (cars, property, equipment)
- Unpaid present entitlements (UPEs) from trusts to corporate beneficiaries
- Table: trigger types with examples

## H2: How Division 7A Loans Work

### H3: The Loan Agreement Requirements (300 words)
- Must be a written, compliant loan agreement
- Benchmark interest rate (updated annually by ATO — currently ~8.27% for FY2025-26)
- Maximum term: 7 years (unsecured) or 25 years (secured over real property)
- Minimum yearly repayment formula: principal + interest amortised over the term
- Key point: missing a single minimum repayment triggers a deemed dividend for the ENTIRE outstanding balance
- Table: example repayment schedule for $100,000 unsecured loan

### H3: The Benchmark Interest Rate (150 words)
- Set by the ATO each financial year
- Based on the RBA indicator lending rate for small business
- Must be charged and paid (not just accrued) — common trap
- Internal link opportunity: → annual rate tracker page

### H3: Unpaid Present Entitlements (UPEs) (250 words)
- Trust distributes income to a corporate beneficiary
- Company doesn't actually pay out the distribution
- ATO treats the UPE as a "loan" under Div 7A from FY2022-23 onwards (PS LA 2010/4 updated)
- This is the most common Div 7A trap for family trust structures
- Key point: affects almost every family group with a corporate beneficiary

## H2: The ATO Trap — Common Mistakes

### H3: Mistake 1: No Written Agreement (200 words)
- Verbal "arrangements" between director and company
- Even if repayments are being made, no written agreement = deemed dividend
- Fix: execute a compliant agreement before the earlier lodgement date

### H3: Mistake 2: Missing Minimum Repayments (200 words)
- One missed payment triggers deemed dividend on the entire balance
- Partial payments don't count — must meet the minimum calculated amount
- Common in cash-flow-tight years — directors defer repayments and forget the Div 7A consequence

### H3: Mistake 3: Interposed Entity Loans (200 words)
- Loan from Company A → Company B → Shareholder
- ATO "looks through" interposed entities to find the ultimate recipient
- Section 109T extends Div 7A to indirect arrangements
- Key point: restructuring to avoid Div 7A is itself an avoidance arrangement

### H3: Mistake 4: Forgetting Asset Usage (150 words)
- Director uses company car, lives in company-owned property, or uses company assets
- Market-value use charge must be declared or it's a deemed dividend
- Commonly missed by sole-director companies

### H3: Mistake 5: Ignoring UPEs Post-2022 (200 words)
- Pre-2022: UPEs were largely tolerated as "sub-trust" arrangements
- Post-2022: ATO finalised guidance — UPEs must be placed on compliant Div 7A terms
- Transitional rules apply but many businesses haven't acted

## H2: Consequences of Getting It Wrong

### H3: Deemed Dividends and Tax Impact (250 words)
- Deemed dividend is assessable income to the shareholder at their marginal tax rate
- No franking credits attached (unlike normal dividends)
- Potential top marginal rate: 47% (including Medicare levy)
- Example: $200,000 loan with no agreement → $200,000 unfranked deemed dividend → ~$94,000 tax liability
- ATO penalties: up to 75% of the tax shortfall for intentional disregard
- Interest charges on top

### H3: ATO Compliance Focus Areas in 2025-26 (200 words)
- Private company loans (always on the ATO's radar)
- Trust-to-company UPEs (active compliance programme)
- Property and asset usage arrangements
- Data matching: ATO cross-references company financials with director tax returns
- Key point: ATO has AI-assisted compliance tools now — discrepancies are flagged automatically

### H3: Retrospective Loan Agreements (200 words)
- Can be put in place before the "lodgement day" of the income year
- Must meet all Div 7A requirements (rate, term, minimum repayments)

- Common clean-up strategy for smaller amounts
- Key point: only works if done before the lodgement day
