## H2: What Is the R&D Tax Incentive?

### H3: Legislative Framework (200 words)
- Industry Research and Development Act 1986 (repealed) → Tax Incentive under Division 355, ITAA 1997
- Administered jointly by AusIndustry and the ATO
- Key point: Not a grant — it's a tax offset applied after lodgement

### H3: Refundable vs Non-Refundable Offset (250 words)
- Aggregated turnover < $20M → 43.5% refundable offset (18.5% above company tax rate)
- Aggregated turnover $20M–$50M → non-refundable offset with intensity-based premium
- Table: offset rates by turnover bracket
- Key point: Refundable offset means you get cash back even with no tax liability

## H2: Who Is Eligible?

### H3: Entity Requirements (200 words)
- R&D entity incorporated in Australia (or foreign with Australian permanent establishment)
- Must be registered with AusIndustry before lodging the tax return
- Cannot be an exempt entity (trusts, partnerships — with exceptions)
- $20,000 minimum R&D expenditure threshold (or use an RSP)

### H3: Industries That Qualify (Beyond Tech) (300 words)
- Construction: new building methods, materials testing, engineering solutions
- Manufacturing: process improvements, quality control innovations
- Agriculture: crop/soil experimentation, pest management R&D
- Professional services: software tools, methodology development
- Key point: "R&D" ≠ "technology" — systematic investigation is the test

## H2: Core vs Supporting R&D Activities

### H3: What Counts as Core R&D (250 words)
- Section 355-25 criteria: experimental activities with unknown outcome
- Must follow systematic investigation methodology
- Must generate new knowledge (not applying known solutions)
- Examples by industry

### H3: Supporting Activities (200 words)
- Section 355-30: activities directly related to core R&D
- "Dominant purpose" test
- Data collection, prototype construction, trial production runs
- Common mistake: claiming supporting activities without a linked core activity
