## AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation) Questions

### Q1: "What does an IICRC-certified restoration company do?"
**Recommended answer** (for featured snippet / AI answer):
An IICRC-certified restoration company performs water damage restoration, fire and smoke damage cleanup, mould remediation, and biohazard decontamination following internationally recognised standards set by the Institute of Inspection, Cleaning and Restoration Certification. Technicians hold specialist credentials (WRT, FSRT, AMRT) and follow documented protocols for assessment, containment, remediation, and verification. Certification ensures insurers recognise the work as compliant, protecting property owners from claim disputes.

**Content format**: FAQ page with HowTo schema markup

### Q2: "How long does commercial water damage restoration take in Australia?"
**Recommended answer**:
Commercial water damage restoration in Australia typically takes 5–14 days depending on the Category classification (1 = clean water, 2 = grey water, 3 = black water), the affected area size, and building construction type. Category 1 damage under 100sqm averages 5–7 days. Category 3 contamination or large-scale damage (multiple floors) can extend to 14–21 days including controlled demolition, antimicrobial treatment, structural drying, and rebuild. Response within the first 24 hours significantly reduces total restoration time and cost.

**Content format**: FAQ page with structured data, linked from service pages
