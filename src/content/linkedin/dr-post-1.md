When a burst main flooded three levels of a Brisbane CBD office tower at 11pm on a Friday, the building manager had one question:

"Can we be open Monday?"

Here's how we made it happen.

Within 90 minutes of the call, our rapid-response crew was on-site with commercial-grade extraction units, industrial dehumidifiers, and thermal imaging cameras. By 2am, we had mapped every affected zone and begun controlled demolition of unsalvageable materials.

Saturday was about speed-drying. We deployed 14 desiccant dehumidifiers and 40 air movers across all three levels, monitoring moisture readings every two hours. Our project managers coordinated trades for plasterboard replacement and carpet re-laying simultaneously — no waiting for one job to finish before the next began.

Sunday, the final inspection confirmed moisture levels were within Australian Standard AS 4654.2 thresholds. Fresh paint was drying. New carpet tiles were down.

Monday morning, 200 staff walked into a fully operational workspace. Total insured loss: $2.1M. Downtime: zero business days.

This isn't luck. It's process, preparation, and having the right gear staged before disaster strikes.
