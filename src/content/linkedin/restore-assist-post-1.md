The average Australian property insurance claim takes 67 days to settle.

With a dedicated restoration manager? 22 days.

Here's why the gap is so massive.

**1. Documentation starts on day one, not day thirty.**
Most policyholders don't realise their insurer needs scope-of-works documents, moisture readings, and photographic evidence in specific formats. Without guidance, they submit incomplete paperwork — and the clock resets every time.

**2. Trades coordination eliminates dead time.**
A restoration manager sequences demolition, drying, rebuilding, and final inspection as a single workflow. Without one, you're waiting for the plumber to finish before calling the plasterer, who's booked out for three weeks.

**3. Insurer communication stays professional.**
Claim disputes spike when policyholders communicate emotionally rather than technically. A restoration manager speaks the insurer's language — scope codes, MICA categories, and AS/NZS compliance references — which keeps assessors moving.
