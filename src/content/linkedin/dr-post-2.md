Hiring a restoration contractor after property damage is stressful. Most people sign the first company that shows up.

That's a mistake. Ask these five questions first.

**1. "Are your technicians IICRC-certified?"**
The Institute of Inspection, Cleaning and Restoration Certification is the industry standard. If they can't show current credentials, walk away. Your insurer may reject claims for work done by uncertified operators.

**2. "Can you provide a detailed scope-of-works before starting?"**
A professional contractor will assess the damage, document it with moisture readings and thermal imaging, and present a written scope before any demolition begins. "We'll work it out as we go" is a red flag.

**3. "Do you communicate directly with my insurer?"**
Contractors who handle insurer liaison save you weeks of back-and-forth. Ask whether they submit progress reports, scope updates, and variation requests on your behalf.

**4. "What's your average completion time for this type of damage?"**
Experienced contractors have benchmarks. A 50sqm water damage in a commercial tenancy should take 5–7 days, not three weeks. If they can't give you a number, they haven't done enough of them.

**5. "Do you guarantee your work?"**
Written warranties on workmanship and materials are non-negotiable. Verbal assurances are worthless when mould appears six months later.
