Your commercial cleaner says they can handle mould remediation.

But are they IICRC-certified to do it?

If the answer is no, your business is exposed. Here's why credentials matter more than quotes.

**1. Liability protection.**
If a non-certified contractor performs water damage restoration and the problem recurs, your insurer can deny the follow-up claim. IICRC certification (the global standard for cleaning and restoration) demonstrates the work was performed to internationally recognised protocols. That's your evidence trail.

**2. Health and safety compliance.**
Mould remediation, biohazard cleanup, and fire damage restoration involve hazardous materials governed by Safe Work Australia codes of practice. IICRC-certified technicians are trained in containment, PPE requirements, and air-quality monitoring. Uncertified operators guess.

**3. Scope accuracy saves money.**
Certified technicians assess damage using standardised methodologies — moisture mapping, psychrometric calculations, and drying-time projections. This means the scope-of-works matches the actual damage, not a padded estimate or an undercooked one that leads to callbacks.

At CCW, every technician holds current IICRC certification in their specialisation — Water Damage Restoration (WRT), Fire and Smoke Restoration (FSRT), or Applied Microbial Remediation (AMRT).
