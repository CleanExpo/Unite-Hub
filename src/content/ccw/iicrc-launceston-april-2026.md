## Course Overview

The IICRC WRT certification is the global standard for water damage restoration professionals. This intensive 2-day course in Launceston delivers the complete WRT curriculum in a compressed format — ideal for Tasmanian and southern Australian professionals who can't travel to mainland multi-day courses.

Delivered by accredited IICRC instructors with extensive Australian field experience, including work in Tasmania's cooler, high-humidity environments where desiccant drying and structural cavity techniques are particularly critical.

Participants receive the official IICRC WRT certification upon passing the end-of-course examination — recognised by insurers, loss adjusters, and restoration firms across Australia and internationally.

**Note:** This is a 2-day intensive. Expect longer days (8:00 AM – 6:00 PM) compared to the standard 3-day format. Prior reading of pre-course materials (provided upon registration) is strongly recommended.

## 5 Key Learning Outcomes

### 1. Water Damage Science and Psychrometrics
Understand the physics of evaporation, humidity, temperature, and vapour pressure. Learn to read psychrometric charts and design effective drying systems — with specific attention to Tasmanian conditions where cooler temperatures require different equipment strategies than subtropical Queensland.

### 2. IICRC S500 Standard Application
Master the S500 Standard: water damage categories (1, 2, 3), classes of water damage (1–4), restoration vs replacement criteria, contamination protocols, and documentation requirements for Australian insurance claims.

### 3. Equipment Selection, Placement, and Monitoring
Specify and deploy dehumidifiers (refrigerant and desiccant), air movers, air scrubbers, and moisture monitoring equipment. Particular focus on desiccant dehumidifiers — essential in Tasmania's cooler climate where refrigerant units lose efficiency below 15C.

### 4. Moisture Detection and Measurement
Hands-on training with pin-type moisture meters, non-invasive meters, thermo-hygrometers, and infrared cameras. Correct measurement techniques for timber (including Tasmanian hardwoods), plasterboard, concrete, and masonry.

### 5. Health, Safety, and Contamination Control
Category 2 and Category 3 water handling, PPE requirements, antimicrobial treatments, containment procedures, and WHS obligations. Covers Tasmanian-specific considerations including heritage building restoration constraints.
