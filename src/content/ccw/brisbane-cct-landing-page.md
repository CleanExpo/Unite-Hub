## 3 Benefit Bullets

- **Industry-recognised credential** — The IICRC CCT is the global standard for commercial cleaning. It's the qualification insurers check before approving restoration contractors and the credential property managers require in tender documents.

- **Hands-on equipment training** — This isn't a webinar. You'll operate commercial extractors, rotary machines, air movers, and chemical application systems under instructor supervision. You leave with practical competence, not just theory.

- **Career acceleration** — Certified technicians access higher-value contracts, command better rates, and qualify for advanced IICRC certifications (WRT, FSRT, AMRT) that lead into the restoration industry.

## Course Outline — 3-Week Schedule

### Week 1: Foundations (5–7 March 2026)
- **Day 1** — Industry overview, IICRC standards framework, workplace health and safety, PPE requirements
- **Day 2** — Cleaning chemistry: pH scale, surfactants, solvents, oxidisers, and surface compatibility
- **Day 3** — Fibre identification, carpet construction types, and pre-inspection protocols

### Week 2: Practical Skills (12–14 March 2026)
- **Day 4** — Hot water extraction: equipment setup, operation, and maintenance
- **Day 5** — Low-moisture and encapsulation cleaning methods, hard floor care fundamentals
- **Day 6** — Upholstery cleaning, spot and stain removal, odour counteraction techniques

### Week 3: Advanced Application + Exam (19–21 March 2026)
- **Day 7** — Scope-of-works documentation, customer communication, quoting and job costing
- **Day 8** — Supervised practical assessment: full cleaning workflow on commercial site
- **Day 9** — IICRC CCT exam review, practice questions, and formal examination

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need any prior experience to enrol?**
A: No. The IICRC CCT is the entry-level certification. It's designed for people new to the industry as well as experienced operators seeking formal credentials. All practical training starts from fundamentals.

**Q: Is the IICRC CCT recognised by Australian insurers?**
A: Yes. IICRC is the internationally recognised standard for cleaning and restoration. Major Australian insurers (IAG, Suncorp, QBE, Allianz) reference IICRC certification in their approved contractor requirements.

**Q: What happens if I don't pass the exam?**
A: You'll receive feedback on areas for improvement and can resit the exam at a subsequent CCW training event at no additional exam fee. Most participants pass on their first attempt — the course is structured to prepare you thoroughly.

**Q: Can I attend individual weeks or do I need to complete all three?**
A: The CCT certification requires completion of the full course and examination. All three weeks are necessary. However, training days are structured to accommodate participants who need to manage work commitments — contact us to discuss your situation.

**Q: What should I bring?**
A: Closed-toe shoes (mandatory for practical sessions), notepad and pen, and photo ID for exam registration. All equipment, materials, PPE, and catering are provided by CCW.
