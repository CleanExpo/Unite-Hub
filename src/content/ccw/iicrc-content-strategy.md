# CCW IICRC Dual-Win Organic Content Strategy — UNI-1122

**Business**: Catastrophe Cleanup Works (CCW)
**Objective**: Capture both sides of the IICRC market — (a) tradespeople seeking certification, (b) property managers/insurers seeking certified contractors
**Date**: 04/03/2026

## 10 Evergreen Content Pieces

### 1. "How to Get IICRC Certified in Australia — Complete Guide (2026)"
- **Target keyword**: "IICRC certification Australia"
- **Search intent**: Informational
- **Format**: Long-form blog post
- **Word count**: 3,000–3,500
- **Audience**: Tradespeople (primary)
- **Key angle**: Step-by-step pathway from zero to certified — courses, costs, timelines, renewal requirements. Position CCW as the training provider that walks you through it. Include comparison table of certification levels (WRT, FSRT, AMRT, CCT).

### 2. "Why Your Insurer Requires IICRC-Certified Restoration Contractors"
- **Target keyword**: "IICRC certified restoration contractor"
- **Search intent**: Commercial investigation
- **Format**: Blog post
- **Word count**: 1,800–2,200
- **Audience**: Property managers, insurers, facilities managers
- **Key angle**: Risk mitigation angle — uncertified work leads to denied follow-up claims, liability exposure, and non-compliant remediation. Include real-world scenarios (anonymised) where certification status affected claim outcomes.

### 3. "IICRC WRT Certification: What Water Restoration Technicians Actually Learn"
- **Target keyword**: "IICRC WRT certification course"
- **Search intent**: Informational
- **Format**: Blog post + embedded video summary
- **Word count**: 2,000–2,500
- **Audience**: Tradespeople considering WRT
- **Key angle**: Curriculum deep-dive — psychrometrics, moisture mapping, drying science, equipment selection. Demystify the course content so tradespeople know exactly what to expect. Include day-by-day breakdown.

### 4. "How to Choose an IICRC-Certified Cleaner for Your Commercial Property"
- **Target keyword**: "IICRC certified cleaner near me"
- **Search intent**: Commercial
- **Format**: Blog post with checklist download
- **Word count**: 1,500–1,800
- **Audience**: Property managers, body corporates, facilities managers
- **Key angle**: Verification checklist — how to confirm credentials, what questions to ask, red flags to watch for. Position CCW as the benchmark. Include downloadable PDF checklist as lead magnet.

### 5. "IICRC Certification Cost in Australia — Fees, Courses, and What's Included"
- **Target keyword**: "IICRC certification cost Australia"
- **Search intent**: Commercial investigation
- **Format**: Blog post with pricing table
- **Word count**: 1,500–2,000
- **Audience**: Tradespeople (primary), business owners budgeting training
- **Key angle**: Transparent pricing breakdown — course fees, exam fees, renewal costs, travel/accommodation for regional attendees. Compare value against uncertified competition (higher rates, insurer preference, fewer callbacks).

### 6. "Mould Remediation Compliance: What Australian Property Managers Must Know"
- **Target keyword**: "mould remediation compliance Australia"
- **Search intent**: Informational/commercial
- **Format**: FAQ-style blog post (FAQPage schema)
- **Word count**: 2,000–2,500
- **Audience**: Property managers, strata managers, insurers
- **Key angle**: Safe Work Australia obligations, tenant health liability, when DIY becomes negligent, IICRC AMRT as the compliance benchmark. Include state-by-state regulatory summary.

### 7. "From Carpet Cleaner to Restoration Specialist: Career Pathways with IICRC"
- **Target keyword**: "restoration technician career Australia"
- **Search intent**: Informational
- **Format**: Blog post + infographic
- **Word count**: 1,800–2,200
- **Audience**: Tradespeople, career changers
- **Key angle**: Career progression narrative — entry via CCT, upskill to WRT/FSRT, specialise in AMRT, move into project management. Include salary range estimates and demand indicators. Position CCW training as the launchpad.

### 8. "The Hidden Cost of Hiring Uncertified Restoration Contractors"
- **Target keyword**: "uncertified restoration contractor risks"
- **Search intent**: Commercial investigation
- **Format**: Blog post
- **Word count**: 1,500–1,800
- **Audience**: Property managers, insurers, business owners
- **Key angle**: Cost of failure — denied insurance claims, recurring damage, health liability, legal exposure. Contrast with certified contractor outcomes. Data-driven where possible (industry stats on callback rates, claim disputes).

### 9. "IICRC FSRT: Fire and Smoke Restoration Training — What to Expect"
- **Target keyword**: "IICRC fire smoke restoration course"
- **Search intent**: Informational
- **Format**: Blog post + video testimonial
- **Word count**: 2,000–2,500
- **Audience**: Tradespeople with existing WRT looking to upskill
- **Key angle**: Course content deep-dive — char vs ash removal, ozone treatment, thermal fogging, content cleaning, odour counteraction. Day-by-day schedule, prerequisites, and post-certification opportunities.

### 10. "Commercial Cleaning Standards in Australia: Why IICRC Sets the Benchmark"
- **Target keyword**: "commercial cleaning standards Australia"
- **Search intent**: Informational
- **Format**: Authoritative guide (pillar page)
- **Word count**: 2,500–3,000
- **Audience**: Both — tradespeople understanding why it matters, and hirers understanding what to demand
- **Key angle**: Position IICRC as the gold standard alongside Australian Standards (AS/NZS). Compare with competing certifications. Explain how standards translate to measurable outcomes (moisture thresholds, air quality readings, documentation requirements).

## Distribution Channels

### LinkedIn (primary B2B channel)
- Publish summary post with key takeaway for every blog article
- Tag relevant industry bodies (IICRC, RIA, ACRA) where appropriate
- Share in groups: Property Management Australia, Restoration Industry Association, Facility Management Association
- Post frequency: 2x/week (1 original + 1 repurposed)

### Website Blog (ccw.com.au/blog)
- Full articles published here as canonical source
- Schema markup on every post (Article, FAQPage, HowTo as appropriate)
- Internal linking strategy: every post links to at least 2 other pieces in the content hub
- Lead magnets: certification checklist (piece #4), career pathway infographic (piece #7)

### Google Business Profile Posts
- Shortened versions (150 words max) of each article posted as GBP updates
- Focus on local keywords: "IICRC certified [city]"
- Post frequency: 1x/week
- Include CTA to full article on website

### Email Newsletter (monthly)
- Curate top 2 articles per month into newsletter
- Segment: tradespeople list vs property manager list
- Personalise subject lines by segment
