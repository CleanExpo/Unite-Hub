## Course Overview

The IICRC WRT certification is the global standard for water damage restoration professionals. This intensive 3-day course covers the science, methodology, and practical skills required to perform water damage restoration in accordance with the IICRC S500 Standard for Professional Water Damage Restoration.

Delivered by accredited IICRC instructors with decades of field experience in Australian conditions, this course equips technicians with the knowledge to manage water damage jobs from initial assessment through to verified completion.

Participants receive the official IICRC WRT certification upon passing the end-of-course examination — recognised by insurers, loss adjusters, and restoration firms across Australia and internationally.

---

## 5 Key Learning Outcomes

### 1. Water Damage Science and Psychrometrics
Understand the physics of evaporation, humidity, temperature, and vapour pressure. Learn to read psychrometric charts and use them to design effective drying systems for Australian climate conditions — including subtropical (Brisbane, Cairns), temperate (Melbourne, Hobart), and arid (inland) environments.

### 2. IICRC S500 Standard Application
Master the S500 Standard end-to-end: water damage categories (1, 2, 3), classes of water damage (1–4), restoration vs replacement decision criteria, contamination protocols, and documentation requirements. Understand how the standard applies to insurance claims and regulatory compliance in Australia.

### 3. Equipment Selection, Placement, and Monitoring
Learn to specify and deploy dehumidifiers (refrigerant and desiccant), air movers, air scrubbers, and moisture monitoring equipment. Calculate equipment requirements using the specific drying goals method. Understand when to use injection drying, heat drying, and specialty drying techniques for complex structures.

### 4. Moisture Detection and Measurement
Hands-on training with pin-type moisture meters, non-invasive meters, thermo-hygrometers, and infrared cameras. Learn correct measurement techniques for timber, plasterboard, concrete, and masonry. Understand measurement limitations and how to interpret readings in context.

### 5. Health, Safety, and Contamination Control
Category 2 and Category 3 water handling protocols, personal protective equipment requirements, antimicrobial treatment application, containment and negative air procedures, and worker health monitoring. Covers Australian WHS obligations relevant to restoration work.
