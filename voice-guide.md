## Brand Voice Summary

**Tone**: Direct, confident, helpful
**Persona**: Trusted business advisor (not salesperson)
**Audience**: Business owners & managers (tradies, service providers, B2B)
**Goal**: Explain benefits in plain English, help customers succeed

## Core Principles

### 1. **Clear Over Clever**

Always choose the clearest wording, even if it's more basic.

| ‚ùå Clever | ‚úÖ Clear |
|----------|---------|
| "Leverage our sophisticated engagement intelligence" | "See who's most interested" |
| "Orchestrated workflow automation" | "Send follow-ups automatically" |
| "Actionable insights derived from behavioral patterns" | "Know when to follow up" |

### 2. **Specific Over Generic**

Replace vague claims with concrete numbers and examples.

| ‚ùå Generic | ‚úÖ Specific |
|-----------|-----------|
| "Improve engagement" | "3x faster response rate" |
| "Better lead management" | "Find your hottest 10 leads in 30 seconds" |
| "Advanced analytics" | "See which messages get opened" |
| "AI-powered scoring" | "Automatically ranks leads by interest" |

### 3. **Customer Problem Over Product Feature**

Start with what matters to them, not what you built.

| ‚ùå Feature-First | ‚úÖ Problem-First |
|----------------|-----------------|
| "Our CRM integrates with Gmail" | "Never miss a reply in email chaos" |
| "Multi-channel automation" | "Keep all conversations in one place" |
| "Real-time notifications" | "Get alerted when a hot lead replies" |
| "Lead scoring algorithm" | "Know who's ready to buy" |

### 4. **Action-Focused Over Passive**

Use active voice and action verbs.

| ‚ùå Passive | ‚úÖ Active |
|-----------|----------|
| "Workflows can be created" | "Create workflows in seconds" |
| "Is integrated with Gmail" | "Connects directly to Gmail" |
| "Scoring is determined by AI" | "AI automatically scores leads" |

### 5. **Honest Over Hyped**

No superlatives, no promises you can't keep.

| ‚ùå Hype | ‚úÖ Honest |
|--------|---------|
| "Revolutionary AI that transforms businesses" | "Automates your follow-up emails" |
| "The best CRM on the market" | "Built for service companies like yours" |
| "Guaranteed to 10x your revenue" | "Most customers respond 40% faster" |

## Writing Rules

### Sentence Structure

**Keep sentences short** (avg 12-15 words)

| ‚ùå Long | ‚úÖ Short |
|--------|---------|
| "Through the use of advanced machine learning algorithms that analyze customer engagement patterns, our system can predict the optimal time to reach out to prospects, thereby maximizing conversion rates and improving overall business outcomes." | "AI finds the right time to follow up. Get 3x more responses." |

**Use periods, not commas**

| ‚ùå Comma Chain | ‚úÖ Periods |
|---------------|----------|
| "Our system analyzes emails, identifies intent, scores leads, and notifies you of hot opportunities" | "Our system identifies hot leads. Scores them automatically. Alerts you instantly." |

**Active voice first**

| ‚ùå Passive | ‚úÖ Active |
|-----------|----------|
| "Responses should be reviewed daily" | "Review responses every morning" |
| "It is recommended that follow-ups be automated" | "Automate follow-ups to save time" |

### Word Choices

**Use these words** (warm, direct):
- See, know, get, find, create, send, track, discover, explore
- Fast, easy, simple, clear, smart, powerful
- You, your, us, we, together

**Avoid these words** (jargon, corporate):
- Leverage, utilize, orchestrate, synergize, paradigm
- Solution, platform, ecosystem, vertical
- Actionable, scalable, agile, robust, seamless
- Innovation, transformation, disruption

**Numbers over vague claims**:
- ‚úÖ "3x faster responses"
- ‚ùå "Much faster responses"
- ‚úÖ "40% improvement in conversion"
- ‚ùå "Significant improvement in conversion"
- ‚úÖ "Respond to 100 leads in 1 hour"
- ‚ùå "Handle massive volume easily"

### Punctuation

**Use contractions** (sounds friendlier):
- "We've automated" not "We have automated"
- "Don't worry" not "Do not worry"
- "You're covered" not "You are covered"

**Use em-dashes for emphasis** (better than commas):
- "Get alerts when leads reply‚Äîeven at 2am"
- "Automate campaigns‚Äîsave 5 hours per week"

**Use colons for lists** (clearer structure):
- "Three ways to find hot leads: check their email opens, see reply speed, view engagement score"

## Formatting & Structure

### Headlines

**Rule**: First 10 words matter most (mobile screen width)

| ‚ùå Long & Vague | ‚úÖ Short & Specific |
|---|---|
| "Manage Your Contacts More Effectively" | "Find hot leads in 30 seconds" |
| "Improved Email Campaign Performance" | "3x more replies with AI follow-ups" |
| "Dashboard Overview Page" | "Your hottest leads, right now" |

### Subheadings

**Rule**: Answer "Why should I care?"

| ‚ùå Feature Name | ‚úÖ Benefit |
|---|---|
| "Contact Management System" | "Keep track of everyone, automatically" |
| "Campaign Analytics Dashboard" | "Know what's working (and what isn't)" |
| "Lead Scoring Engine" | "Never waste time on cold leads again" |

### Body Text

**Rule**: 1 idea per sentence, 3-5 sentences per paragraph

```
Good structure:
- Sentence 1: What is this?
- Sentence 2: Why should I care?
- Sentence 3: How do I use it?
- Sentence 4: What's the payoff?
```

### Lists

**Rule**: Use action verbs, parallel structure

| ‚ùå Weak | ‚úÖ Strong |
|--------|---------|
| ‚Ä¢ Automation<br/>‚Ä¢ Detailed analytics<br/>‚Ä¢ Gmail integration<br/>‚Ä¢ Multiple users | ‚Ä¢ Automate follow-ups<br/>‚Ä¢ See what's working<br/>‚Ä¢ Connect your Gmail<br/>‚Ä¢ Invite your team |

## Testing Your Copy

### Readability Checklist

- [ ] **Pass the 10-word test**: First 10 words make sense as headline?
- [ ] **One idea per sentence**: Can each sentence stand alone?
- [ ] **Active voice**: Mostly "you do X" not "X is done"?
- [ ] **Specific numbers**: Any vague claims ("more", "better", "faster")?
- [ ] **No jargon**: Would a service business owner understand?
- [ ] **Short sentences**: Average under 15 words per sentence?
- [ ] **Contractions**: Sounds conversational ("you're" not "you are")?

### Grammar Checklist

- [ ] Consistent verb tense (mostly present tense)
- [ ] Parallel structure in lists
- [ ] No double spaces
- [ ] Proper em-dashes (‚Äî not --)
- [ ] No ALL CAPS except acronyms
- [ ] Oxford comma used consistently

### Brand Tone Checklist

- [ ] Confident without being cocky?
- [ ] Helpful without being sales-y?
- [ ] Direct without being rude?
- [ ] Friendly without being cheesy?

## Voice Consistency Checklist

Before publishing any copy, ask:

1. **Is it honest?** No over-promising, no buzzwords?
2. **Is it clear?** Would a non-tech person understand?
3. **Is it specific?** Numbers, examples, concrete outcomes?
4. **Is it actionable?** What does the user do next?
5. **Is it friendly?** Confident but not corporate?
6. **Is it concise?** Every word earns its place?
7. **Is it consistent?** Same tone across all pages?

## Tools for Writing Better Copy

### Before you write:
- Read 3 competitor examples
- Note what works (and what doesn't)
- Identify the customer problem
- Think about the specific benefit

### While you write:
- Say it out loud first
- Write in short sentences
- Use specific numbers
- Start with the benefit

### After you write:
- Remove jargon (Hemingway Editor)
- Check readability (aim for Grade 6-7 level)
- Read it aloud
- Have someone read it cold (do they understand?)
- Test it (A/B test headlines if possible)

## Q&A

**Q: Should we use "I" or "we"?**
A: Use "you" for everything. "You'll save hours..." not "We'll help you save hours..."

**Q: How formal should it be?**
A: Friendly but professional. Like talking to a trusted advisor, not a friend.

**Q: Can we use humor?**
A: Sparingly, and only if it serves the message. No forced jokes.

**Q: What about different locales/regions?**
A: Stick with Australian English (colour, organisation) and local references where relevant.

**Q: Should we update this guide?**
A: Yes. Every 3 months, review copy samples and update examples.
