## 1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

### What We're Building
A distinctive visual brand identity for Unite-Hub, a marketing automation CRM targeting **Australian service business owners** (tradies, electricians, plumbers, builders aged 35-55).

**Goal**: Make the product feel less corporate and more approachable‚Äîlike a tool designed specifically for Australian tradies, not a generic SaaS app.

### The Problem We're Solving
- Current design feels "Silicon Valley" (modern but generic)
- Target audience is skeptical of tech, wants simplicity
- Need to build trust through relatable, authentic visuals
- Australian tradies respond to practical, no-nonsense design

### Who Will Use This
Australian service business owners who spend most of their time on job sites, not in offices.

## 3. VISUAL STYLE & TONE

### Overall Aesthetic
**Cartoon-Realistic Blend** with Australian Context

Think: Australian tradie magazine cover illustrations, not Silicon Valley startup aesthetics.

### Color Palette
**Primary**: Teal #0d9488 (current brand color)
**Secondary**: Warm Gray #78716c (current brand color)
**Accents**:
- Success green: #10b981
- Warning orange: #f97316
- Error red: #ef4444

### Character Style
- **Line Weight**: Consistent 2-3px strokes
- **Faces**: Warm, friendly expressions (not too cartoonish)
- **Clothing**: Practical work wear (hi-vis, tool belts, work boots)
- **Hairstyles**: Diverse, modern, Australian
- **Hands**: Visible hands showing action/emotion

### Cultural Context (IMPORTANT)
- Australian tradies are practical, skeptical of hype
- Images should show **real work** (fences, plumbing, electrical, construction)
- Show tools and equipment specific to Australian trades
- Avoid corporate/office aesthetics‚Äîthis is a field operation business
- Include Australian landscape context where relevant (bushland, suburban homes)
- Avoid stereotypes‚Äîshow diverse ages, ethnicities, genders

### Tone
- Professional but approachable
- Authentic, not overly stylized
- Practical, showing real work situations
- Warm and encouraging (not aggressive)

## 4. SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

### Technical Specifications

**File Format**: SVG (scalable, can adjust colors programmatically)
**Backup Format**: PNG at 2x resolution (for export)

**Sizing Requirements**:
- Hero illustrations: 1200√ó800px (16:10 ratio)
- Mobile illustrations: 1080√ó1440px (3:4 ratio, portrait)
- Portrait/headshots: 600√ó600px (1:1 ratio)
- Team illustrations: 1200√ó600px (2:1 ratio)

**Color Output**:
- SVG should use named color variables (e.g., `fill="var(--teal-600)"`)
- PNG should use exact colors from design-system.css
- Ensure all text elements are proper text elements (not paths)

### Brand Guidelines to Follow
- Use the 5 core voice principles: Clear, Specific, Benefit-focused, Active, Honest
- Every illustration should tell a story (not just pretty pictures)
- Each illustration should support an emotional connection with Australian tradies
- Avoid corporate jargon in any illustration labels or callouts

### Australian Context Requirements
‚úÖ Show real tools: Spanners, electrical equipment, plumbing fixtures, etc.
‚úÖ Show real vehicles: Utes/utilities (common in Australia), not generic vans
‚úÖ Show Australian landscape: Suburban homes, gum trees, Australian weather
‚úÖ Use Australian English spelling and terminology
‚úÖ Reflect Australian work culture: High-visibility clothing, practical attitude
‚ùå Don't use: Generic Silicon Valley imagery, overly futuristic tech, corporate office settings
